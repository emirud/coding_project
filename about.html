About Me

My name is Emma, or Emilija, how my Lithuanian friends and family know me. I'm from a tiny town in the middle of Lithuania, a central-north European country with a complicated language and history. A story too long to tell. The most important thing to know - we smile very rarely, seem very mean and welcome people into our homes like family. Weird, right?
I am 22 years old and study in Bath, UK, far away from home. I consider myself to be cosmopolitan and a global citizen, so I wanted to move to a different country to experience all of the beauty of diversity and the opportunity to experience new cultures and traditions. That worked out great, as I made fantastic friends in uni from all over the world.
I work part-time basically all the time I am in uni to support myself, so I am hella stubborn and have lots of grit to keep on working on my dreams. The single most important reason why this blog is even alive, if I may say so myself. I felt like this was a story I wanted to tell, so here we go!
